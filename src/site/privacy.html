Last updated 23rd April 2026

This privacy policy sets out how we use and protect any information that you provide when you use this website.

Who We Are

The data controller responsible for your information is Solirius Reply Accessibility Team. You can contact us regarding your data at accessibility@solirius.com.

Information We Collect & Why

We collect your Name and Email Address.

Purpose: To manage your registration for our event/webinar on May 18th 2026 and to send you necessary event-related communications (reminders and links).

Lawful Basis (UK GDPR Article 6): We process this data based on your Consent. You provide this consent by signing up for the event.

Cookies

We use one functional cookie on our website.

  • Purpose: This cookie checks if you have already registered for the event to improve your user experience.
  • Tracking: This is a non-tracking, session-based cookie. It does not collect personal identifiers or monitor your behavior on other sites.

How We Protect Your Data

In accordance with our "Accountability" obligations, we have implemented strict technical safeguards:

  • Storage: All data is kept in a secure, password-protected environment.
  • Access Control: We enforce Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all platforms used to store or process your data.
  • Password Security: We use unique, high-entropy passwords for all services, managed via a secure password manager.

Third-Party Links

Our website may link to external sites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those websites. We recommend you read the privacy policies of any external site you visit, as our policy does not apply to those sites.

Data Retention

We follow the principle of Data Minimisation. We will only hold your personal data for as long as is necessary for the event.

Deletion Schedule: All personal data collected for the May 18th 2026 event will be permanently deleted within 30 days of the event's conclusion (by June 17, 2026).

Your Rights

Under the UK GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You can change your mind and ask us to stop using your data at any time.
  • Right of Access: You can ask for a copy of the information we hold about you.
  • Right to Erasure: You can request that we delete your data before the 30-day automatic deletion period.
  • Right to Complain: If you are unhappy with how we use your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) (www.ico.org.uk)

Contact Information

To exercise any of your rights or ask questions about this policy, please contact: accessibility@solirius.com
